My Philosophy

A Methodical Learning Process

My approach to learning is methodical and foundational. I typically begin with high-level resources like university lectures to grasp the core concepts of a new topic. From there, I dive into official documentation and tutorials to understand the practical specifics of implementation. I believe that building this strong theoretical foundation is the key to effective problem-solving, as it allows me to reason about why a certain approach might be better than another.

Striving for Depth

While the tech market often rewards breadth, my personal goal is to cultivate depth. I am driven by a desire to understand the 'why' behind the tools I use. This principle guides me to spend extra time exploring the fundamental mechanics of an algorithm or system, as I believe this is the path to becoming a more capable and insightful engineer.

Writing Clean and Maintainable Code

An ML model is only as effective as the code that implements it. I focus on writing software that is clean, readable, and well-documented. While I am still early in my career, I practice the habits of modular design and clear commenting. My goal is to produce work that is not only correct, but also easy for others to understand, review, and build upon.
